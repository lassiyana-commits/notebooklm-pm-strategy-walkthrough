# 📖 Glossary

No prior tech or PM experience needed — here's every term used in this repo, in plain English.

| Term | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|
| **NotebookLM** | A free Google tool that reads documents you give it and answers questions using *only* that material — like a research assistant who's read exactly what you handed them, nothing more, nothing invented. |
| **Gemini** | Google's general-purpose AI chat assistant — good for open-ended creative and analytical thinking, not limited to specific uploaded documents. |
| **Notebook** (in NotebookLM) | A project workspace. Think of it like a labeled binder for one specific topic. |
| **Source** | Any document, article, or link you upload into a notebook. NotebookLM will only "know" what's inside your sources. |
| **Note** | A piece of text you save inside a notebook — either something you typed, or an AI answer you chose to keep. Notes can later be promoted into sources. |
| **Prompt** | The instruction or question you type to an AI tool. Good prompts are specific about the role, the goal, and the format you want back. |
| **Report** (in NotebookLM) | A longer, structured document NotebookLM generates for you by pulling together the sources/notes you selected. |
| **Product Manager (PM)** | The person on a team responsible for deciding *what* a product should do and *why*, based on user needs and business strategy — not the person who writes the code. |
| **Product Strategy** | A reasoned plan for what a company should build and why it will win against competitors. |
| **SWOT Matrix** | A simple 4-box way of analyzing something: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. |
| **Ecosystem Lock-In / "Stickiness"** | A business strategy where a company makes its products work so well *together* that customers don't want to switch to a competitor's product, because they'd lose that combined benefit. |
| **On-Device / Local Processing** | AI processing that happens directly on your phone or laptop, instead of being sent to a company's remote servers ("the cloud"). Usually faster and more private. |
| **Cloud AI** | AI processing that happens on a company's remote servers rather than your own device. |

Don't see a term you're unsure about? Open an issue or just ask — that's the fastest way we'll know to add it here.
